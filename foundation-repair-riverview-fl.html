Foundation warning signs

Foundation problems homeowners commonly ask about

Foundation concerns can be stressful because they may connect to structure, soil, drainage, moisture, and long-term property value. Start with what you can see and request a local evaluation.

Cracks & Gaps

Wall cracks, stair-step cracks, slab cracks, separation around doors or windows, and visible gaps that seem to be growing.

Settling & Uneven Floors

Sloping floors, doors that stick, windows that no longer close correctly, or changes that suggest movement over time.

Moisture Concerns

Water pooling near the home, damp areas, recurring moisture, erosion, and signs that drainage may be affecting the foundation.

Inspection & Next Steps

Professional evaluation before repairs, before buying or selling, or before deciding whether drainage or structural work is needed.

When to request help

Signs you should not ignore

  • Cracks are widening, spreading, or appearing in several rooms.
  • Doors or windows suddenly stick, separate, or stop closing properly.
  • Floors feel uneven, sloped, soft, or different from nearby rooms.
  • Water regularly pools near the slab after heavy Florida rain.
  • You see erosion, sinking soil, or recurring moisture near the home.
  • You need to understand whether the issue is structural, drainage-related, or both.

FAQ

Foundation repair questions

How do I know if a crack is serious?

Cracks that widen, spread, appear with sticking doors or uneven floors, or show up after moisture problems should be evaluated by a qualified professional.

Can drainage problems affect a foundation?

Yes. Poor drainage, pooling water, erosion, and grading issues can contribute to moisture and movement concerns near a slab or foundation.

Should I request foundation or drainage help first?

If you see structural signs such as cracks or movement, foundation help may be the right first request. If the main issue is standing water, a drainage contractor may also be relevant.
